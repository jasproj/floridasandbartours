About Key West

Key West sits at the end of the Overseas Highway, 90 miles closer to Havana than to Miami. The water is shallow, warm, and protected by the Florida Keys reef — the third-largest barrier reef in the world — which is why the area runs nearly year-round on charters: snorkel trips to Western Sambo and the Eastern Dry Rocks, sandbar runs to Marvin Key and Woman Key, sunset sails on schooners that have been on the water since the 1930s. Most of the operators we partner with launch from Stock Island Marina or the Historic Seaport, both of which are walking distance from Old Town.

If you're a first-time visitor, the typical Key West day is two activities: a morning snorkel or sandbar trip, lunch back at the dock, then a sunset cruise out of Mallory Square. Mid-day water gets crowded between November and April when cruise ships are in town; if you can book a 9 AM departure, you'll have the reef largely to yourself.

What to know before you book

  • High season runs December through April; book sandbar and reef trips 1-2 weeks ahead during peak weeks
  • Mid-summer water temperature averages 84°F (29°C) — wetsuits not needed, but reef-safe sunscreen is mandatory in Florida Keys waters
  • Most charter pickups are at Stock Island Marina Village or the Historic Seaport (Sunset Pier area) — both 5-10 min from cruise ports
  • Hurricane season is June-November; September-October is peak risk, but most operators offer free reschedule with 24-hour notice
  • Family-friendly tours typically allow ages 4+ for sandbar trips and 6+ for snorkel; check operator policies on each tour page
  • Cell service is patchy at the offshore reefs — bring photos to the boat if you want a specific snorkel spot
