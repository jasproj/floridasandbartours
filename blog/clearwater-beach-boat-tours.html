Clearwater Beach Boat Tours: Dolphins, Sandbars, and Day Trips

Pinellas County, Florida • Updated March 2026

The Clearwater Beach area encompasses a stretch of Gulf Coast that includes Madeira Beach, Belleair Bluffs, Treasure Island, and St. Pete Beach. Boat tours in this corridor have some of the best dolphin watching in Florida, access to Egmont Key, and a range of options from $29 dolphin cruises to private charters for groups.

Most of the tour activity is concentrated around Madeira Beach and the Johns Pass area, which has several competing operators and boat types. Here's a breakdown of what's running and what you get for the price.

Hubbard's Marina — Madeira Beach

Hubbard's Marina is one of the largest and most established boat tour operations in the Clearwater area. They operate multiple vessels and run daily dolphin cruises, the Egmont Key ferry, and fishing charters out of the Johns Pass waterway in Madeira Beach. It's a big operation with set schedules and online booking, which makes it easy to plan around.

Egmont Key Ferry

Hubbard's Marina — Madeira Beach
FerrySnorkelIsland Trip
$45 per person

Round-trip ferry to Egmont Key Island at the mouth of Tampa Bay. The island has a working lighthouse, Civil War fort ruins, sea turtle nesting beaches, and a snorkeling spot along the south shore. The ferry drops you and picks you up — you have free time on the island. Bring your own snorkel gear if you want to use the water.

Sunshine Scenic Tours — Madeira Beach

Sunshine Scenic Tours operates out of Madeira Beach and runs a few different boat types, including a pirate ship and a dedicated dolphin quest boat. They're aimed at a family and entertainment crowd, with the pirate ship being particularly popular with kids.

Captain Clearwater — Private Charters (Belleair Bluffs)

Captain Clearwater operates private boat charters from Belleair Bluffs, which is on the Intracoastal Waterway between Clearwater and Largo. Their tours are all private — no sharing the boat with strangers — and they cover the local waterways, barrier islands, and dolphin areas based on what the group wants to do. Good for small groups that want flexibility.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [sunscreen](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

Glass-Bottom Kayak Tour (Treasure Island)

Sharkey's Glass Bottom Tours in Treasure Island runs a kayak tour through mangrove tunnels and to a local sandbar using clear-bottom kayaks. It's not a traditional boat tour, but it's a unique water experience that gives you a look at the shallow Gulf floor in a way that you can't get from a motorized boat.

What's the Best Option in the Clearwater Area?

It depends heavily on what you're after and who you're going with:**Pro tip:** Bring a [cooler](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

p>
  • Lowest cost dolphin cruise: Dolphin Quest ($29) or Hubbard's Dolphin Watching Cruise ($29.95)
  • Best island day trip: Egmont Key Ferry from Hubbard's ($45)
  • Best for kids/families: Pirate Ship from Sunshine Scenic ($42)
  • Best private experience: Captain Clearwater 2 or 4-hour private charter
  • Most unique: Sharkey's Glass Bottom Kayak tour ($62)

The Madeira Beach / Johns Pass area has the most options concentrated in one place — you can walk between operators and compare boats and schedules in person, which is worth doing if you're flexible on which tour you book.

Parking: Johns Pass Village in Madeira Beach has paid parking in lots adjacent to the marina area. Arrive early on weekends — the lots fill by mid-morning during peak season. Belleair Bluffs has street parking near the marina. Treasure Island has public lots near the beach and launch area.
