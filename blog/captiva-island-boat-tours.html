Captiva Island Boat Tours: Day Trips, Dolphin Cruises, and Island Hopping

Lee County, Florida • Updated March 2026

Captiva Island sits north of Sanibel, connected by a bridge at the island's midpoint. It's smaller, less developed, and borders both Pine Island Sound on the east and the Gulf of Mexico on the west. The waters around Captiva — Pine Island Sound, Redfish Pass, and Captiva Pass — have some of the best barrier island hopping access in Southwest Florida, with Cayo Costa State Park just four miles north by water.

Most of the boat tour options in the Captiva / Sanibel area depart from nearby Fort Myers Beach or from marinas on the mainland side — Captiva doesn't have a large commercial marina, so tour operators work from nearby launch points. Here's a breakdown of what's available for getting on the water in this part of Southwest Florida.

Cabbage Key Island Getaway

Cabbage Key is a small private island in Pine Island Sound between Fort Myers Beach and Captiva. It has a historic inn and restaurant built in the 1930s — reportedly the inspiration for Jimmy Buffett's "Cheeseburger in Paradise" — and is only accessible by boat. Sunset Jet Ski Rental & Tours runs a boat tour from Fort Myers Beach that makes the run to Cabbage Key as a day trip destination.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [waterproof bag](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=YOUR-TAG-20) for the best experience.

Fishing Charters from Fort Myers Beach

The waters around Captiva and Pine Island Sound are also productive fishing grounds — snook, redfish, and tarpon are all caught in the Sound and at Redfish Pass. Good Time Charters runs half-day fishing charters from Fort Myers Beach for anglers who want to target the Captiva-area fishery.

What to See Around Captiva by Boat

The island chain north of Captiva is one of the more remote and scenic stretches of the Southwest Florida coast:

    **Pro tip:** Bring a [motion sickness bands](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=YOUR-TAG-20) for the best experience.

  • Cayo Costa State Park: A pristine 9-mile barrier island north of Captiva accessible only by boat. Has an undeveloped Gulf beach and strong shelling. The park has primitive camping for those who want to stay overnight.
  • Redfish Pass: The tidal pass at the north end of Captiva is one of the most productive fishing spots in the area and has high dolphin activity due to baitfish concentration at the pass.
  • Captiva Pass: North of Cayo Costa, the pass between Cayo Costa and North Captiva has sea turtle nesting areas and good shelling at low tide.
  • Pine Island Sound: The shallow estuary between the barrier islands and Pine Island has extensive sea grass beds that support manatees, dolphins, and an array of wading birds.

Best time to visit: Winter (December-March) is peak season in the Captiva area. The weather is dry and comfortable, wildlife is concentrated, and the crowds on Captiva itself are manageable compared to summer. Fishing is good year-round, with tarpon season running May through July if you're targeting that species specifically.

Getting to Fort Myers Beach for Captiva Area Tours

Fort Myers Beach is on Estero Island, connected to the mainland by a bridge on US-41. The marinas for Good Time Charters and Sunset Jet Ski are in the northern part of Fort Myers Beach near the Times Square area. There's public parking in the Fort Myers Beach garage on Estero Boulevard, and paid parking in several lots near the marina district. Traffic on the single-bridge access can back up on weekends — allow extra time in winter and spring.
