Florida Private Boat Charters: What You Get and What It Costs

Statewide Guide • Updated March 2026

Private boat charters give you a boat, a captain, and the ability to design your own day on the water without a fixed group tour itinerary. The captain knows the local waters and will suggest stops and routes based on conditions, but the final say is yours. You're not waiting on other passengers to finish shelling; you're not stuck at the dolphin viewing spot for exactly 20 minutes because that's what the group tour allows.

The premium for private charters is real — you're paying for the whole boat regardless of how many people are on it. But for groups of 6+ people, private charter prices per person often fall within range of group tour prices while giving you significantly better experience quality. Here's a breakdown of what's available across Florida with real pricing.

When Does a Private Charter Make Financial Sense?

Here's the math on when to book private vs. group:

  • Group of 2-4 people: Group tours are usually better value unless the experience quality matters a lot to you
  • Group of 6-8 people: Private charters often hit per-person parity with group tours while giving you a private experience
  • Group of 10+: Private charters are almost always better value per person, often cheaper than group tours
  • Special occasion (anniversary, proposal, birthday): Private charter regardless of group size — the experience difference is significant
  • Kids under 8: Private charter allows for more flexibility on timing, stops, and pace — worth the premium

Tip for booking private charters: Ask the captain directly what the current conditions favor. A good captain will tell you honestly if the tides aren't right for sandbar access on your date, or if a different stop would be better than what you asked for. Captains who push back or make suggestions based on conditions are typically the better ones — they're prioritizing your experience over just collecting the booking fee.
