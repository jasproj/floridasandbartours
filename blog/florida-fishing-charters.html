Florida Fishing Charters: Half-Day, Full-Day, and Offshore Options

Statewide Guide • Updated March 2026

Florida is one of the top sportfishing destinations in the country for a simple reason: the coastline gives you access to an enormous range of fisheries from close range. Inshore anglers target redfish, snook, and tarpon in the estuaries and backwaters. Nearshore trips hit the grouper reefs and amberjack ledges within a few miles of shore. Offshore trips run to the deep water for mahi-mahi, wahoo, sailfish, and marlin.

Fishing charter pricing ranges from $85 for a jon boat rental at a local wildlife area to $1,800+ for a full-day private offshore yacht charter. Most private half-day nearshore charters for 1-6 people run $450-700. Here's a breakdown of what's available across Florida, excluding the Miami-Dade/Keys area.

Port Canaveral: Offshore Fishing

Port Canaveral on Florida's Atlantic Coast is one of the best jumping-off points for offshore fishing in Florida. The Atlantic continental shelf is relatively close to shore here, giving boats faster access to deep water than in most Gulf Coast ports. Dirty Dolly Fish Company operates multiple offshore charter vessels out of Port Canaveral.

Fort Walton Beach / Destin: Offshore Charter Fishing

The Destin / Fort Walton Beach area is consistently ranked as one of the top sportfishing ports in the Gulf of Mexico. The DeSoto Canyon, a deep underwater canyon where the Gulf of Mexico drops from 100 to over 1,000 feet, is about 30 miles offshore and produces exceptional billfish, wahoo, and mahi-mahi fishing. Destin Private Yachts runs charter trips that combine sightseeing with fishing.

Choosing a Florida Fishing Charter

The most important factors in choosing a fishing charter are the target species, the departure location, and whether you want a private or shared charter.

  • Inshore / backwater (bays, estuaries): Targets snook, redfish, tarpon, trout, flounder. Typically private charters for 2-4 people. Calm water, no seasickness risk. Good for families and non-anglers who want to watch.
  • Nearshore (3-30 miles): Targets grouper, snapper, amberjack, cobia on reef and ledge structure. Most half-day charters operate in this range. Moderate ocean conditions.
  • Offshore (30-80+ miles): Targets mahi-mahi, wahoo, sailfish, marlin, and deep grouper. Full-day or longer trips. Requires more sea legs — 3-4 foot ocean swells are common on offshore trips.

What's typically included: Most Florida fishing charters include rods, reels, tackle, and bait. Fish cleaning is sometimes included and sometimes an additional charge — ask before you book. Fishing licenses are usually covered by the charter's commercial license. You're typically responsible for your own food, drinks, and tips (10-20% is standard for a good charter captain).
