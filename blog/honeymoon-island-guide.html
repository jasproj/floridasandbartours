Honeymoon Island State Park Guide

Guide • Updated April 2026

Honeymoon Island State Park near Clearwater is a premier Florida destination featuring pristine beaches, excellent shelling, wildlife viewing, picnic facilities, and access to nearby Caladesi Island. The park offers diverse outdoor activities suitable for families, couples, and nature enthusiasts.

Honeymoon Island Beaches & Water Activities

The island features beautiful Gulf-facing beaches with calm waters and fine sand. The beach near the parking area offers easy access and facilities. The northern beach, accessed via short walk, is quieter and excellent for shelling. Swimming is generally safe year-round, though water temperature varies seasonally. Kayaking and paddleboarding are popular for exploring backwater areas and spotting wildlife.

Shelling & Wildlife Viewing

Honeymoon Island is excellent for shelling year-round, particularly after storms. Species include coquina clams, lightning whelks, and sand dollars. Wildlife viewing opportunities include dolphins, manatees (winter), and diverse shorebirds. Osprey nests and sea turtle nesting areas (seasonally) are visible from beaches. Early morning visits provide best wildlife activity and fewer crowds.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [sunglasses](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

Park Facilities & Getting There

The park charges $2/person entrance fee ($5 per vehicle). Facilities include parking, bathrooms, picnic areas, and shower stations. The causeway drive to the island offers scenic views. No food service exists on the island except vending machines — bring provisions or eat before arrival. Dogs are allowed in certain areas. Hurricane season (June-November) occasionally causes closures.

FAQs

What's the best time to visit Honeymoon Island?

October-April offers pleasant temperatures and good shelling. Summer is hot and more crowded. Winter provides the most wildlife viewing.

Can I camp on Honeymoon Island?

No camping on the island itself, but nearby Caladesi Island via ferry offers campsites. Other nearby parks have camping.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [waterproof bag](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

How do I get to Caladesi Island from Honeymoon Island?

A ferry departs from Honeymoon Island's north end (seasonal operation). Check current schedules when planning your visit.

Is there a time limit for staying at Honeymoon Island?

The park closes at sunset. Most visitors spend 3-5 hours for beaching, shelling, and picnicking.
