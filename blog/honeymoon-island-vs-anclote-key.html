Honeymoon Island vs Anclote Key

Comparison Guide • Updated April 2026

Honeymoon Island State Park and Anclote Key are two pristine Gulf barrier islands offering distinct beach and boating experiences in the Tampa Bay region. Honeymoon Island features developed infrastructure, ranger programs, and easy beach access alongside the Caladesi ferry operation. Anclote Key remains more remote and wild with superior shelling and fewer facilities. Both islands showcase Florida's natural beauty with excellent swimming, wildlife viewing, and coastal adventure. Understanding the differences helps you choose the perfect island destination.

Accessibility & Infrastructure

Honeymoon Island is easily accessible by car via State Road 590, featuring ample parking, restrooms, picnic areas, and visitor amenities. A developed state park with marked trails, ranger programs, and visitor facilities. Ideal for day-use recreation with minimal planning required. Ferry services to Caladesi Island depart from Honeymoon Island, offering convenient island-hopping. Anclote Key is accessible only by private or tour boat (no ferry service), creating more solitude but requiring boat rental/tour booking. Less developed with minimal facilities, offering wilderness adventure rather than recreation convenience. Choose Honeymoon for easy access; Anclote for adventure and solitude.

Beach Quality & Shelling

Honeymoon Island features white sand beaches with moderate shelling opportunities, especially after storms. The 2.5-mile beach provides excellent swimming and sunbathing. Water clarity and quality are good but affected by bay influence. The developed beach handles larger crowds well. Anclote Key delivers exceptional shelling, consistently ranked among Florida's best shelling beaches. White sand, abundant shells, and pristine conditions dominate. Water clarity is excellent with superior Gulf influence. The remote nature means fewer crowds and more solitude. Beach excellence favors Anclote; accessibility favors Honeymoon.

Wildlife & Nature

Honeymoon Island features diverse wildlife within developed park infrastructure. Dolphins, sea turtles (nesting season), birds, and dolphins sightings occur reliably. Mangrove trails provide nature exploration. Ranger programs enhance educational experiences. Anclote Key excels in wildlife viewing with less human disturbance. Pristine habitat supports thriving dolphin populations, sea turtle nesting sites, and diverse bird species. The undeveloped nature preserves ecosystem integrity. Both islands offer excellent nature experiences; Honeymoon adds interpretation and education, Anclote provides authentic wilderness.

Cost & Trip Planning

Honeymoon Island costs $6 vehicle entry fee plus optional Caladesi ferry ($8-15/person). Total cost is very low, making it budget-friendly. No boat rental required if visiting Honeymoon only. Same-day decision possible without advance booking. Anclote Key requires tour operator booking ($40-80 per person) or private boat rental ($200-400+). Advance booking typically required during peak season. Total costs are higher but include professional guidance and transportation. Choose Honeymoon for budget simplicity; Anclote for guided experience or adventure value.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Honeymoon Island for families wanting easy beach access, day-trippers with limited planning time, those seeking amenities and services, interest in Caladesi Island hopping, or budget-conscious travel. Honeymoon suits casual vacationers and those prioritizing convenience. Choose Anclote Key for serious shellers, nature enthusiasts, wildlife photographers, those seeking solitude, or adventure-oriented travelers. Anclote rewards planning and commitment with pristine experiences. Many visitors explore both—Honeymoon morning beach visit, Anclote afternoon guided tour for shelling and wildlife.

Perfect Day Combo: Start at Honeymoon Island morning for easy beach access and Caladesi ferry. Afternoon/late day: book Anclote Key tour for shelling and sunset. Combines convenience with pristine wilderness in one memorable day.

FAQs

Can I visit both Honeymoon Island and Anclote Key in one day?

Yes, absolutely. Morning at Honeymoon Island ($6 entry, optional Caladesi ferry). Afternoon Anclote Key tour (book in advance). Total cost $40-100 depending on choices, creating comprehensive island-hopping experience.

What's the best shelling season at these islands?

September-April offers peak shelling after storms. Both islands have shells year-round, but fall and winter provide the best variety and abundance. Anclote Key consistently outperforms Honeymoon for shelling quantity.

Are there accommodations near these islands?

Yes. Honeymoon Island has nearby hotels, resorts, and vacation rentals in Dunedin and Clearwater area. Anclote Key is remote with limited nearby lodging. Visitors typically day-trip or stay in nearby Tarpon Springs/Dunedin for Anclote access.

Can young children visit Anclote Key?

Yes, tour operators accommodate families with children. Boat rides are 20-30 minutes. Shallow beaches suit wading. Bring proper sun protection, water, and snacks (none available on island). Check with operators for age/safety requirements.
