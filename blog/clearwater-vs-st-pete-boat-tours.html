Clearwater vs St Pete Beach Boat Tours

Guide • Updated April 2026

Clearwater and St. Pete are both iconic Florida beach destinations with excellent boat tour options. Each offers unique experiences: Clearwater excels in family-friendly activities and dolphin viewing, while St. Pete provides urban beachside charm with diverse tour options. Understanding the differences helps you choose the perfect destination for your boat tour adventure.

Clearwater Beach Boat Tours

Clearwater is famous for its pristine beach and calm intracoastal waters. The area has excellent dolphin populations and numerous shallow-water viewing opportunities. Most tours depart from Clearwater Beach or nearby Belleair Bluffs, with easy parking and beach access. Tours tend to be family-focused with shallow-water routes suitable for all ages.

St. Pete Beach Boat Tours

St. Pete Beach offers a wider variety of boat tour experiences, from urban sunset cruises to fishing charters and wildlife tours. The area has more commercial development with more dining and entertainment options before or after your tour. Tours tend to be more diverse, catering to different interests beyond just wildlife viewing.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [sunglasses](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

Which Location is Right for You?

Choose Clearwater if you want reliable dolphin viewing, family-friendly activities, and scenic intracoastal cruises. Choose St. Pete if you want more variety in tour types, urban beach atmosphere, and additional activities around the dock area. Both are worth visiting — consider your primary interests to decide.

FAQs

Which has better dolphin viewing — Clearwater or St Pete?

Clearwater edges ahead with more consistent morning dolphin sightings. St. Pete has dolphins too, but viewing depends more on specific route choices.

Is one location more family-friendly?

Both are family-friendly. Clearwater is slightly more geared toward families with calm intracoastal tours. St. Pete offers more diverse activities for different age groups.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [waterproof bag](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

How far apart are Clearwater and St Pete?

About 30 miles apart — roughly 45 minutes to an hour drive depending on traffic.

Can I visit both locations in one trip?

Yes, many visitors combine both locations over a week-long vacation for varied experiences.
