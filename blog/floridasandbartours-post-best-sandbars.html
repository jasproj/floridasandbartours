Best Sandbars in Florida: Shell Key, Crab Island, Anclote Key, and More

Florida's Gulf coast has more accessible sandbars than any other US state. They range from protected nature preserves to full-on party destinations. Here are the five most visited, with what makes each different.

1. Shell Key Sandbar - Gulfport / St. Pete

Distance: 20-30 min from Gulfport Marina
Depth: 1-3 feet
Protection status: Shell Key Preserve (protected)
Tour price: $50/person

Shell Key Preserve is a protected barrier island in southern Pinellas County - the most consistently calm and clear Gulf coast sandbar accessible from Tampa Bay. The island's Gulf-facing side has shallow sandy sandbars with minimal boat traffic (it's a nature preserve - no party scene). The water is typically clear enough to see stingrays and small fish gliding through the shallows.

Shell Key is also a migratory bird sanctuary - herons, pelicans, and shorebirds nest on the island edges. Dolphins are common on the transit from Gulfport, especially in the morning. No motorized vehicles or amplified music on the island - it's one of the quieter Gulf coast sandbar experiences.

2. Crab Island - Destin

Distance: 10-15 min from Destin waterfront
Depth: 1-3 feet
Character: Social/party scene weekends
Tour price: $79/person

Crab Island is Florida's most famous sandbar - and the most social. On a summer weekend afternoon, dozens of boats anchor in the shallow water, vendor boats circulate selling food and drinks, and hundreds of people wade between the vessels. It's a floating block party on a sandbar, which is either exactly what you're looking for or the opposite of it depending on your travel style.

Crab Island is technically in Choctawhatchee Bay, protected from the Gulf. The water is calm and shallow (1-3 feet), warm year-round, and clear. Weekday mornings are significantly quieter if you want the sandbar without the crowd.

3. Anclote Key - Near Tarpon Springs

Distance: 3 miles off Tarpon Springs (30-40 min by boat)
Depth: 1-4 feet on sandbar side
Status: Anclote Key Preserve State Park

Anclote Key is the Gulf coast's best-kept sandbar secret near Tampa Bay. It's a state park island 3 miles off Tarpon Springs - accessible only by boat - with a Gulf beach, lighthouse, and surrounding sandbars that see a fraction of Shell Key's traffic. The water is typically clear, the Gulf beach is wide and uncrowded, and the sandbar on the Gulf-facing side is consistently calm.

There are no tour operators running regular scheduled trips to Anclote Key - it's primarily reached by private charter or boat rental from Tarpon Springs. If you're renting a boat in the area, this is the destination to request from your captain.

Best for: Visitors with a private boat or who specifically request it from a Tarpon Springs charter. The least-crowded premier sandbar within reach of Tampa Bay.

4. Jupiter Sandbar - Palm Beach County

Distance: Short boat ride from Jupiter Inlet
Depth: 1-3 feet
Coast: Atlantic (not Gulf)
Tour price: $40/person

The Jupiter sandbar is on Florida's Atlantic coast - a different experience from Gulf coast sandbars. Atlantic water is clearer and cooler than the Gulf in most seasons, but more exposed to ocean swell. The Jupiter sandbar is a local gathering spot near Palm Beach County, accessible by boat from Jupiter Inlet and popular with Palm Beach and Fort Lauderdale area visitors.

5. Treasure Island Area (Glass Bottom Kayak)

Distance: Short kayak/paddle from launch point
Type: Mangrove tunnel + sandbar combination
Tour price: $62/person

Not a traditional sandbar, but the most distinctive sandbar experience in the Tampa Bay area: the Glass Bottom Kayak tour in Treasure Island. Transparent-bottom kayaks let you view underwater life while paddling through mangrove tunnels, then you emerge at a local sandbar. It's the only way to see what's below the surface of a Gulf coast sandbar without actually snorkeling.

Frequently Asked Questions
