Tampa Bay Sandbar Tours: Shell Key, Egmont Key, and Beyond

Tampa Bay Area • Updated March 2026

Tampa Bay is one of Florida's largest estuaries, and the coastline that borders it — from Gulfport and St. Pete down through Tierra Verde and Fort De Soto — has some of the most accessible sandbar and island tours in the state. You don't have to drive to the Keys or Southwest Florida to spend a day wading on a sandbar. Shell Key Preserve, Egmont Key, and the shallows off Gulfport are all under an hour by boat from most of the St. Pete area.

This guide covers the main sandbar tours operating in Tampa Bay, what each destination offers, and how to pick the right one for your group.

The Main Sandbar and Island Destinations

Shell Key Preserve

Shell Key Preserve is a protected area of barrier islands and shallow flats southwest of Fort De Soto Park. The preserve includes several uninhabited islands with good shelling beaches and a large sandbar that's popular for wading and relaxing. Access is by kayak or small boat — the water is shallow enough that most of the preserve isn't reachable by larger vessels. This makes it genuinely quieter than the more developed beach areas nearby.

Egmont Key

Egmont Key is a barrier island at the mouth of Tampa Bay, about nine miles from St. Pete Beach. It has a lighthouse, Civil War-era fort ruins, and a decent Gulf beach. You can snorkel the rock piles along the south shore. The ferry from Hubbard's Marina in Madeira Beach gets you there in about 20 minutes, and Island Boat Adventures in Gulfport runs a full four-hour tour that combines Egmont Key with shelling and dolphin watching.

Shell Key / Gulfport Area Sandbars

Island Boat Adventures, operating from Gulfport, runs tours to a cluster of sandbars and shallow water areas between the mainland and the barrier islands. The Shell Key Sandbar Sunset Cruise and the 2.5-hour Shell Key Cruise are two of the most popular tours in the entire Tampa Bay area for people who just want to get on the water and see dolphins without a complicated itinerary.

Egmont Key Ferry (Hubbard's Marina)

If you just want to get to Egmont Key without a guided tour, the ferry from Hubbard's Marina in Madeira Beach runs direct trips to the island. The ferry drops you off and picks you up later, giving you free time on the island to explore the beach, lighthouse, and fort. It's cheaper than a full guided tour but you're on your own once you land.

Kayak Tours to Shell Key Preserve

For a quieter sandbar experience, kayaking to Shell Key Preserve from the Tierra Verde launch is one of the better options in the Tampa Bay area. Get Up and Go Kayaking and Coastal Kayak Charters both run guided kayak tours to the preserve, using clear-bottom kayaks that let you see the shallow sandy bottom as you paddle.

Choosing Between Boat Tours and Kayak Tours

The boat tours from Island Boat Adventures cover more ground and get you to Egmont Key faster, but the kayak tours from Tierra Verde give you a quieter, wildlife-closer experience in the preserve. Here's a quick comparison:

  • Best for speed and coverage: Island Boat Adventures 4-hour Egmont Key tour
  • Best budget group tour: Island Boat Adventures 2.5-hour Shell Key Cruise at $49
  • Best for wildlife/kayakers: Get Up and Go Kayaking Shell Key Clear Kayak Tour
  • Best private option: Island Boat Adventures 4-hour private charter at $550
  • Best solo/DIY: Egmont Key Ferry from Hubbard's Marina at $45

Water conditions: Tampa Bay's shallow areas can get choppy in the afternoon when sea breezes kick up. Morning tours — before noon — tend to have calmer water. The kayak tours to Shell Key Preserve are more affected by wind than the boat tours, so check conditions before booking a kayak trip if it's a windy time of year.

Getting to the Launch Points

Island Boat Adventures departs from the Gulfport Municipal Marina, about 10 minutes from downtown St. Pete. Hubbard's Marina is in Madeira Beach at the south end of the Johns Pass area — there's parking in the marina lot. The Tierra Verde kayak launches (Get Up and Go, Coastal Kayak) are at the Fort De Soto Park entrance, about 30 minutes from downtown St. Pete. Sharkey's Glass Bottom Tours launches from Treasure Island.
