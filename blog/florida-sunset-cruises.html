Florida Sunset Cruises: A Guide to the Best Routes and Operators

Statewide Guide • Updated March 2026

Florida's Gulf Coast faces west, which means the entire stretch from Naples to the Panhandle gets unobstructed sunset views over open water every night the sky is clear. Watching the sun drop into the Gulf from a boat is genuinely good — better than from shore because you get 360-degree sky and no buildings or trees blocking the horizon.

Sunset cruise options range from $40 tiki boat floats to $1,800 private yacht charters. The right option depends entirely on what you're after: a casual BYOB boat experience, a structured guided cruise with a captain narrating on local landmarks, or a fully private yacht with catering. Here's a breakdown of what's available across the state.

How to Pick a Sunset Cruise

The sunset itself is the same regardless of which boat you're on — what differs is the experience around it. Here's a quick framework:

  • Want cheap and casual: Tiki boat sunset cruises in Naples ($50-59) or the Shell Key Sandbar Sunset Cruise ($50) in Gulfport
  • Want a private experience for a couple: Sand Dollar Boat Tours' Port Royal private sunset ($650) or Destin Private Yachts' Magical Sunset/Harbor Lights ($360)
  • Want a private yacht for a larger group: Destin Private Yachts' Miss B Haven sunset charters ($650-975)
  • On a budget in South Florida: Sea Rocket Fort Lauderdale sunset cruise ($39.99)
  • Want wildlife with your sunset: The Ten Thousand Islands Sunset Eco-Tour from Goodland

Check the sunset time before you book. Florida's sunset time shifts from around 5:30 PM in December to 8:30 PM in June. Most sunset cruises depart 1.5-2 hours before actual sunset. Verify the departure time against the day's actual sunset time — a mismatch can mean you're watching the sunset during your return transit rather than at anchor in a scenic spot.
